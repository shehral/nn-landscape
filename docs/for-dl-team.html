Patterns this build

  • Open-source document pipeline covers every layer simultaneously: Unstructured (extraction), RAGFlow (orchestration), and UltraRAG (MCP-connected RAG) all trend in the same build, forming a complete open-source substitute for the Nanonets extraction-to-workflow stack.
  • GRPO fine-tuning and IDP-benchmark evaluation of document VLMs are now single-framework operations: ms-swift enables GRPO on IDP-competitive models while lmms-eval and evalscope provide standardized DocVQA/ChartQA evaluation, forming an end-to-end competitive model development pipeline.
  • Orchestration platform fragmentation continues: Dify, RAGFlow, UltraRAG, and LangChain all trend simultaneously, each bundling document parsing defaults. API-level distribution to any single platform is insufficient; connector coverage across the ecosystem is the relevant unit.
  • IDP-proximate VLMs are moving toward local deployment: Ollama lists Kimi-K2.5, GLM-5, and DeepSeek; LLaVA-OneVision-1.5 enables derivative training. Enterprise data-residency arguments for cloud-based document extraction weaken as capable models become locally runnable.
  • Benchmark toolchain standardization is accelerating without OCR-3 participation: lmms-eval and evalscope trend together for the third or more consecutive build, both covering DocVQA and ChartQA. Competitors can publish standardized IDP-adjacent comparisons that structurally omit OCR-3.
