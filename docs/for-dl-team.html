Patterns this build

  • Open-source document extraction is commoditizing the OCR-3 /parse surface: MinerU, OCRmyPDF, RAGFlow, Firecrawl, Tesseract, and Paperless-ngx all trend in a single cycle, each framing their product as 'PDF to LLM-ready data' — OCR-3's own positioning phrase. The six items span MIT/Apache licensing and are backed by organizations ranging from Shanghai AI Lab to the Apache Foundation.
  • Agent-layer platforms (Dify, BiSheng, UltraRAG, Genkit, AutoGPT) are consolidating the workflow tier above extraction, and none have confirmed Nanonets OCR-3 connectors. Five distinct platforms trending simultaneously suggests the orchestration layer is not converging on a winner, which means the connector gap is not self-resolving — each platform independently requires an integration decision.
  • Behavioral hallucination-mitigation paradigms cluster for the first time in three independent items: NVIDIA VSS spatial grounding, video-evaluator evidence anchoring, and SGLang constrained decoding. All three operate without model-internals access, are implementable as post-hoc filters, and are structurally complementary to mechanistic interpretability rather than competing with it.
  • On-device and local VLM inference is accelerating across hardware tiers: Ollama (featuring GLM-5.1, Kimi-K2.6, gpt-oss locally), Qualcomm GenieX (NPU inference), TokenSpeed (new inference engine), and mlx-porting-skill (Apple Silicon quantization) all trend in one cycle. This pattern extends local competitive model access to a wider device class, reducing the cloud-serving cost moat for managed OCR APIs.
  • Pixel-level and spatially-grounded retrieval is emerging as a distinct RAG paradigm: PixelRAG ('the end of web parsing, the beginning of pixel-based RAG') and NVIDIA VSS (spatial evidence citation per extracted value) both operate at the image-region level rather than on OCR-extracted text. If this paradigm gains traction, downstream retrieval workloads may bypass the text-extraction step that OCR-3 currently fills.
