Patterns this build

  • The GRPO fine-tune supply chain for IDP Leaderboard competitors is now feature-complete in open source. ms-swift (GRPO training for Qwen3-VL, InternVL3.5, GLM5.1), evalscope (evaluation), and Ollama (local inference of these exact model families) all trend in the same cycle. Every component needed for an external lab to produce a credible leaderboard submission is publicly available without cloud infrastructure.
  • The all-open-source document AI pipeline now covers every stage without a commercial API. This build surfaces MinerU (extraction), PaddleOCR (OCR), PyMuPDF (PDF parsing), Dify/RAGFlow/BiSheng (orchestration), and lmms-eval/evalscope (evaluation) as a complete, actively maintained stack. The make-vs-buy argument for Nanonets now rests entirely on accuracy and support rather than capability coverage.
  • Agent-layer platforms are consolidating around above-extraction positioning, each bundling its own parsing defaults. Dify, BiSheng, RAGFlow, and LangChain each describe themselves as agentic workflow platforms in terms that overlap with Nanonets Agents; none defaults to Nanonets as the extraction backend. This represents a distribution channel risk independent of OCR-3's accuracy position.
  • lmms-eval and evalscope, maintained by EvolvingLMMs-Lab and ModelScope respectively, are becoming de facto benchmark infrastructure for IDP Leaderboard comparables. The same team (EvolvingLMMs-Lab) maintains both lmms-eval and the NEO native VLM series, creating an integrated benchmark-design-to-training loop that may advantage models built with lmms-eval benchmark design in mind.
  • Two distinct developer access paths are growing without Nanonets coverage: Genkit (Google-backed, enterprise production SDKs in JS/Go/Python, existing connectors for Anthropic/OpenAI/Gemini) and Ollama (open local inference, now listing multiple IDP Leaderboard comparable model families). Together they cover the enterprise developer tier and the open-source researcher tier with no Nanonets connector in either.
