Patterns this build

  • The GRPO fine-tuning supply chain for IDP Leaderboard competitors is now complete in a single trending cycle: ms-swift (GRPO training), lmms-eval (evaluation harness), and LLaVA-OneVision-1.5 (fine-tuning framework) all trend together, meaning an external lab can prepare and submit a competitive document VLM to the IDP Leaderboard with publicly available tooling — a barrier that has dropped from months to weeks.
  • Five above-extraction-layer agentic platforms trend in a single cycle (Dify, LangChain, BiSheng, Flowise, genkit) with no confirmed Nanonets OCR-3 connector in any of them; the distribution gap at the orchestration layer is now a consistent multi-build pattern rather than individual gaps, suggesting it is a structural exposure rather than a sequencing issue.
  • Three retrieval-based hallucination-adjacent paradigms trend simultaneously — ArtSeek (late-interaction corpus retrieval), video-evaluator (spatial grounding of extracted values), and Knowledge-Infused Multimodal Retrieval (generation-time RAG) — the first cycle in which all three appear together, suggesting the retrieval-based alternative to mechanistic interpretability for hallucination detection is attracting concurrent developer attention.
  • Model compression (AngelSlim), local inference (Ollama, RunanywhereAI), and high-throughput serving (SGLang) all trend in a single cycle, indicating the deployment economics of large VLMs are shifting toward on-premise and edge segments — a segment that OCR-3 at 35B MoE cannot currently serve, and where a compressed GRPO-tuned competitive model could close the accuracy gap while running locally.
  • PyMuPDF and paperless-ngx both trend alongside VLM-based tools, confirming the document AI extraction market remains bifurcated between deterministic programmatic parsing and VLM-native approaches; the programmatic layer (PyMuPDF as base) is actively developed and increasingly adopted as the cost-effective tier, while VLM-native APIs (OCR-3) must demonstrate measurable accuracy gains on the tasks where PyMuPDF fails.
