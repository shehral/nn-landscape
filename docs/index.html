  • The open-source document AI stack is complete and co-trending simultaneously: extraction-layer (MinerU, PaddleOCR), orchestration-layer (Dify, RAGFlow, BiSheng, LangChain), and evaluation-layer (lmms-eval, evalscope) tools all appear in the same cycle.   • Two distinct retrieval-based hallucination mitigation approaches (Knowledge-Infused-Multimodal-Retrieval, ArtSeek's late-interaction retrieval) co-trend with a grounded evidence extraction tool (video-evaluator), forming a convergent pattern around non-mechanistic methods for detecting and preventing VLM hallucinations. Three or more consecutive builds now show this item class without a confirmed team stance on whether retrieval-based mitigation is inside the research scope.
  • EvolvingLMMs-Lab maintains both lmms-eval (the multimodal evaluation toolkit used for IDP Leaderboard submissions) and NEO (a native VLM under active development). The same organization designing the benchmark tooling and building competitive models creates a conflict-of-interest risk for benchmark objectivity: architectural choices in NEO may influence what lmms-eval rewards, disadvantaging models not participating in its design process.
  • Model compression (AngelSlim) and high-throughput multimodal serving (SGLang) co-trend with GRPO fine-tuning (ms-swift), indicating optimization pressure across the full large-VLM deployment stack. The combination enables an external lab to fine-tune, evaluate, and compress a competitive VLM for edge deployment in a single toolchain, potentially narrowing the serving-cost advantage of smaller specialized models.
