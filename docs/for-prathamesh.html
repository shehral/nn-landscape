Patterns this build

  • A complete GRPO fine-tune-to-evaluate supply chain for document VLMs is now assembled from three co-trending repositories: ms-swift (fine-tuning), Ollama (serving), and evalscope (evaluation). Any lab can now train, serve, and benchmark a competing document VLM without custom infrastructure, materially lowering the IDP Leaderboard submission barrier.
  • Three agent orchestration platforms -- Dify, BiSheng, and RAGFlow -- trended simultaneously without confirmed OCR-3 connectors. Collectively they represent a significant distribution surface for document-extraction APIs; the absence of native OCR-3 integrations in all three is a recurring pattern across multiple build cycles.
  • Open-source PDF and OCR tooling (MinerU, OCRmyPDF, Tesseract) consistently scores highest in github_trending-only builds, reflecting strong developer demand for free extraction alternatives. This pattern is visible across all github_trending-only builds; it also reflects the source bias of the current infrastructure rather than the full competitive landscape.
  • Two items in this build -- PixelRAG and video-evaluator -- independently surface paradigms that bypass or complement structured extraction rather than competing within it: pixel-native retrieval and grounded-evidence anchoring. Their co-occurrence suggests a nascent research direction of reducing reliance on VLM-generated structured output.
  • IDP Leaderboard-adjacent model families (Kimi-K2.6, GLM-5.1, gpt-oss, Qwen3-VL, Qwen3-Omni) are now all locally servable via Ollama and fine-tunable via ms-swift; the toolchain for producing and distributing competitive document VLMs outside the leaderboard submission process is substantially lower-friction than six months ago.
