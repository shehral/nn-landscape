Patterns this build

  • Open-source document parsers continue to commoditize the extraction layer: MinerU, opendataloader-pdf, and PaddleOCR are all trending simultaneously with identical positioning — PDF/Office to LLM-ready structured data — and all three are free, open-weight alternatives to Nanonets Agentic Data Extraction.
  • Above-extraction orchestration platforms bundle document parsing by default, creating a distribution bypass: BiSheng, RAGFlow, and Dify are all trending with integrated document workflow capabilities; customers selecting any of these full-stack platforms have no structural reason to add a dedicated extraction API.
  • VLM evaluation tooling is proliferating without OCR-3 registry presence being confirmed: evalscope, vlmscope, and crossroute-audit are all trending, covering DocVQA, VQA benchmarks, and hallucination auditing respectively — each is a channel through which competitors can publish comparisons that structurally omit Nanonets.
  • Competitive document VLMs are becoming locally runnable: Ollama's default model list includes GLM-5.1, and ms-swift provides GRPO fine-tuning for Qwen3-VL, GLM-5.1, and InternVL — together they lower the cost of third-party document extraction experiments against OCR-3 to near-zero.
  • This is the eleventh or more consecutive build sourced entirely from github_trending: arXiv, HN, and RSS all returned zero items again, which means the competitive and research signal from academic papers, news, and newsletters remains completely absent and the dashboard's coverage is structurally biased toward production tooling.
