Patterns this build

  • Open-source document extraction stack now spans the full pipeline in a single trending cycle: MinerU (PDF-to-LLM-ready extraction), opendataloader-pdf (AI-ready PDF parsing), OCRmyPDF (Tesseract OCR baseline), and paperless-ngx (document management) all trend simultaneously. A functionally complete open-source substitute for Nanonets Agentic Data Extraction is available across all pipeline stages without requiring a paid API.
  • Two parallel VLM evaluation frameworks — lmms-eval and evalscope — trend simultaneously with overlapping benchmark coverage (DocVQA, ChartQA, IDP-adjacent tasks) and registered support for the same competitive model set (Qwen3-VL, GLM-5.1, InternVL3.5). Converging adoption suggests these frameworks will become the standard channel for publishing third-party VLM benchmark comparisons, including comparisons that could structurally exclude models not yet registered.
  • IDP Leaderboard competitive models are accessible for both local inference (Ollama: GLM-5, Kimi-K2.5, DeepSeek, Qwen) and GRPO fine-tuning (ms-swift: Qwen3-VL, GLM-5.1, InternVL3.5) in the same build cycle. The combination closes the gap between evaluating a competitor model and actively fine-tuning and improving it — a toolchain that was distributed across multiple steps is now accessible as a unified local workflow.
  • Retrieval-based VLM hallucination mitigation appears in two distinct implementations this build (self-reflective-rag, Knowledge-Infused-Multimodal-Retrieval), continuing a pattern visible across multiple prior builds. The retrieval paradigm addresses phantom-row and structural hallucination classes through a different mechanism than mechanistic interpretability, and its repeated trending appearance without a competing interpretability-based project suggests the retrieval approach is gaining broader open-source adoption.
  • Three production orchestration platforms trend in this build — Dify, RAGFlow, and Google genkit — with no confirmed Nanonets connector for any of them. Genkit introduces a new dimension: it is Google-backed with established enterprise distribution and connectors for the three major frontier APIs, making its absence of a Nanonets connector qualitatively different from the community-platform connector gap raised in prior builds.
