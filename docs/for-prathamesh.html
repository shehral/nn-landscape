Patterns this build

  • The open-source document AI stack now covers every pipeline layer in a single trending cycle: MinerU and opendataloader-pdf at extraction, Dify and RAGFlow at orchestration, lmms-eval and evalscope at evaluation, XTuner at fine-tuning training. Individually each has appeared before; their simultaneous appearance indicates a maturing open-source alternative to the full Nanonets stack.
  • Both lmms-eval and evalscope trend simultaneously with overlapping IDP-adjacent benchmark coverage and without OCR-3 in their model registries. Any third-party benchmark publication using either framework will structurally omit OCR-3 from comparisons.
  • Omni-modality serving is diverging from LLM-only serving: vllm-omni and SGLang both trend as frameworks explicitly optimized for multimodal inference, while XTuner targets ultra-large MoE training. The infrastructure layer is specializing at the same time OCR-3 requires it.
  • MCP-native integration is consolidating as a distribution standard: UltraRAG and genkit both trend with MCP or connector-first architectures. Anthropic, OpenAI, and Gemini connectors exist in genkit; Nanonets is absent from both.
  • Ollama's updated headline now names Kimi-K2.5 and GLM-5 — both unconfirmed for document extraction capability — alongside established IDP Leaderboard comparables (Qwen, DeepSeek). Local-inference access to potential leaderboard entrants is expanding before their document-benchmark status is established.
