Patterns this build

  • Complete open-source document AI pipeline trending in a single cycle: MinerU (extraction), opendataloader-pdf (PDF parsing), ms-swift (GRPO fine-tuning), and evalscope (evaluation) are all present. This is the most complete co-occurrence of the open-source stack across 30+ build cycles, representing a zero-cost end-to-end alternative to the Nanonets pipeline.
  • GRPO fine-tuning supply chain now explicitly covers 2026-series IDP Leaderboard successors: ms-swift lists Qwen3.6, GLM-5.1, InternVL3.5, and Qwen3-Omni. Paired with evalscope, the complete fine-tune-to-evaluate pipeline for a GRPO-tuned IDP Leaderboard submission exists in public tooling for every major competitive model family.
  • Five orchestration-layer platforms trend without confirmed Nanonets OCR-3 connectors: Dify, RAGFlow, LangChain, UltraRAG, and Genkit. LangChain's repositioning to 'agent engineering platform' this cycle converts a named integration partner into a concurrent competitor, widening the effective distribution gap.
  • Three distinct post-hoc and retrieval-side hallucination mitigation paradigms appear in a single build: video-evaluator (spatial source-region anchoring), Knowledge-Infused-Multimodal-Retrieval (knowledge injection at generation time), and artseek (late-interaction retrieval over large multimodal corpus). This is the first cycle where all three co-trend, signaling convergent developer interest in mitigation approaches that do not require model internals access.
  • Model compression (AngelSlim), distributed training (NeMo Automodel), GRPO fine-tuning (ms-swift), and serving optimization (SGLang) all trend together. Individually, each addresses one stage of the competitive model lifecycle; together they describe a complete pipeline for producing a compressed, benchmark-optimized document VLM at competitive IDP Leaderboard accuracy and lower serving cost than OCR-3.
