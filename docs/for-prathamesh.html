Patterns this build

  • The complete open-source document AI stack is trending simultaneously in a single build cycle: extraction (Unstructured, opendataloader-pdf, OCRmyPDF), orchestration (Dify, RAGFlow, BiSheng, UltraRAG, LangChain), serving (SGLang, vllm-omni), and training (ms-swift, XTuner, LLaVA-OneVision-1.5). The ecosystem has reached functional completeness as an alternative to managed document-AI APIs.
  • The GRPO fine-tuning supply chain for IDP Leaderboard competitive models is fully assembled in this cycle: ms-swift supports Qwen3-VL, Qwen3-Omni, InternVL3.5, Ovis2.5, and GLM4.5v; XTuner handles ultra-large MoE at OCR-3 scale; LLaVA-OneVision-1.5 provides a community-accessible multimodal training framework. The toolchain barrier to a GRPO-fine-tuned leaderboard submission has been substantially lowered.
  • Three serving frameworks (SGLang, vllm-omni, ax-llm) and one training infrastructure item (NVIDIA-NeMo Automodel) trend in the same cycle, indicating that external labs are completing both training and serving portions of an OCR-3-class deployment pipeline concurrently, not sequentially.
  • Retrieval-based multimodal evidence and hallucination research (ArtSeek, video-evaluator, Knowledge-Infused-Multimodal-Retrieval, cxr-text-bridge-retrieval) trend together for the second consecutive cycle, indicating that retrieval-side approaches to multimodal grounding are maturing into open-source tooling across art, video, general image, and medical imaging domains simultaneously.
  • Above-extraction-layer orchestration platforms (Dify, RAGFlow, BiSheng, UltraRAG, LangChain, AnythingLLM) account for the majority of competitive-axis items this cycle. None carry a confirmed Nanonets ADE connector. The orchestration tier now drives more trending activity than the extraction tier itself, which inverts the typical signal distribution for a document-AI dashboard.
