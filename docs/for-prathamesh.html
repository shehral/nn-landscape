Patterns this build

  • The complete open-source document AI stack is now trending simultaneously: ETL (Unstructured), DMS (paperless-ngx), orchestration (Dify, LangChain), and RAG pipeline (UltraRAG, all-in-rag) all appear in one build cycle. End-to-end document workflows are achievable without proprietary services.
  • Open VLM training frameworks targeting sub-7B multimodal models (TinyLLaVA Factory, LLaVA-OneVision 1.5) are converging toward document-task capability. External labs can now produce IDP Leaderboard-competitive fine-tunes without OCR-3-scale compute.
  • Three production-grade developer frameworks trending this cycle (Genkit, UltraRAG, Dify) lack confirmed first-party or community Nanonets connectors. Developers building on these frameworks default to bundled or API-native extraction alternatives.
  • VLM inference and training infrastructure is maturing in parallel: SGLang (serving, RadixAttention) and NVIDIA NeMo Automodel (distributed training) both trend, reducing the cost and complexity of deploying or fine-tuning large multimodal models.
  • Retrieval-based multimodal hallucination mitigation (knowledge-infused RAG) continues to surface across consecutive builds. The approach is not converging with mechanistic-interpretability methods; the research community is developing it as an independent mitigation path.
