  • Complete GRPO fine-tuning-to-evaluation supply chain for IDP Leaderboard model families trends in one cycle: ms-swift (training), LLaVA-OneVision-1.5 (base model), and evalscope (evaluation) together cover Qwen3.6, GLM-5.1, and InternVL3.5, materially lowering the barrier to a competitive leaderboard submission from months to weeks.
  • OCR-3 is absent from both major VLM evaluation registries while they trend simultaneously: lmms-eval and evalscope both cover DocVQA, ChartQA, and OmniDocBench, and competitive model families (Qwen3-VL, GLM-5.1, InternVL3.5) are registered in both.   • Three independent open-source document parsers — PaddleOCR, MinerU, and opendataloader-pdf — trend in one cycle using LLM-ready or AI-ready data framing identical to Nanonets Agentic Data Extraction, all without a published accuracy comparison anchoring the make-vs-buy argument on a standard benchmark.
  • Three independent spatial-evidence grounding implementations converge in one cycle from different starting points — NVIDIA VSS Blueprint (enterprise video AI), video-evaluator (automated reasoning), and artseek (multimodal retrieval) — each requiring extracted values to cite their source region. The convergence suggests the paradigm is hardening and is directly applicable to phantom-row detection in document extraction without model internals access.
  • Three orchestration platforms — Dify, RAGFlow, and LangChain — trend simultaneously without confirmed Nanonets OCR-3 connectors, while Unstructured.io (their default extraction layer) also trends. Developers adopting these platforms default to Unstructured for extraction, bypassing Nanonets at the distribution decision point.
