Patterns this build

  • Efficiency-first models are outperforming frontier VLMs on structured OCR metrics at multiple parameter scales: PP-OCRv6 (34.5M) beats Qwen3-VL-235B, GPT-5.5, and Gemini-3.1-Pro; Nemotron Nano 2 VL leads OCRBench v2 on single-GPU hardware; FireRed-OCR-2B reaches 92.94% OmniDocBench v1.5. Frontier model parameter count is no longer a reliable proxy for OCR accuracy.
  • The OCR benchmark reference frame is fracturing: LlamaIndex declares OmniDocBench saturated and promotes ParseBench, while NVIDIA introduces OCRBench v2 with a different evaluation methodology. Three concurrent benchmark frameworks with different design philosophies will produce incompatible leaderboards, complicating cross-vendor accuracy claims.
  • Frontier labs are winning named enterprise document AI customers: Ramp deploys Gemini 3.5 Flash for production invoice OCR (Google launch case study), while LlamaIndex's legal-kb reference app extends into Contracts vertical. The competitive threat is now vendor-relationship and enterprise-trust, not just model accuracy.
  • Structural VLM hallucination is coalescing as a defined research subfield with three methodologically distinct interventions published in two months: Format-Constrained GRPO (training-time, FireRed-OCR), Causal Route Gating (inference-time, no retraining), and Transcoder circuit tracing (interpretability-grade prediction). The research community is converging on the same problem from different angles.
  • Frontier model availability is now a documented geopolitical risk: Fable 5's 18-day export control suspension was unannounced and reversed equally abruptly. Enterprise pipelines with frontier API dependencies have no contractual protection against similar interruptions, which creates a residual risk argument for on-premises or self-hosted document extraction.
